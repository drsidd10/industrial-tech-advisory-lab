## Overview

This repository is a portfolio of advanced quantitative models designed for high‑stakes decision‑making in industrial technology investing and M&A.

Each project blends systems engineering thinking, stochastic modelling, and applied financial logic to analyse complex industrial assets—delivering decision‑grade, executive‑level insights for pre‑deal due diligence and post‑merger value creation.

## Why This Lab?

This lab is a personal exploration of how industrial and deep‑technology risks can be made explicit, measurable, and commercially actionable.
Rather than treating technology as a black box, the models here aim to:

- Translate engineering uncertainty into cash, risk, and value terms
- Explore failure modes, not just base‑case outcomes
- Make implicit technical assumptions visible to investors

Principles:
- Reality‑Driven — grounded in how industrial systems actually behave
- Probabilistic Thinking — uncertainty is modeled, not ignored
- Sharable Frameworks — open analyses designed to be explored and critiqued


## Portfolio Contents
Each folder contains:

- **Business Case Notebook** — problem framing, model, simulation, decision insight  
- **Python Model Code** — reusable module for integration  
- **Visuals** — annotated, executive-ready charts  

### Models Included

1) Industrial Ruin Simulator -- Stochastic modelling of manufacturing yield failure and cash‑runway risk

2) Technical‑Debt‑Adjusted Valuation -- Calibration of enterprise value for hidden engineering and scale‑up risk

3) EBITDA Architecture Optimizer -- Quantifying post‑merger engineering decisions that drive margin expansion

4) Carve‑Out Dependency Mapper -- Graph‑based analysis of system and supplier entanglement in separations

5) Patent & Architecture Moat Scorer -- Quantitative assessment of IP defensibility versus competitors
